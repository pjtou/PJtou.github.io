GUNAS AND DOSHAS      (Pierre Touchard)




Gunas are litterally "thread, string" a force that comes during incarnation as Purusha, or Spirit, is becoming involved with Prakriti or Nature.   In Shamkhya philosophy, this movement of the Gunas happen early and involve all stages of matter, as the creation is getting more and more cristallized.  As it were, Shamkhya concepts are imbeded in the early astrology texts of Parasara (Brihat Parasara Hora Shastra) and the Yavana of Spujidhava, 1st century AD.  This suggests that astrology depicts how planets and signs are inbred into the creative process of the Gunas, but also, are part of that creative process of incarnation.   In some translations of Parasara the word Shakti is used for each Guna, showing it to be a creative force.   Bear in mind that planets are termed Grahas in Shamkhya, which are deities, not material planets. 

Parasara (date unknown, but Parasara is purported to be one of the 18 sages who gave astrology to mankind) and Spujidhava are the earliest extant texts giving Gunas and Doshas for each planet. Mantreswhara in 13th century is very close to Parasara.  In this table, in block letter are planets identical for both authors. The text of Spujidhava follows. Some authors derive Vata from Sattva which is how I placed Vata below Sattva, Pitta below Rajas and Kapha below Tamas logically. 

To better follow, Phelgm is the traditional translated word for Kapha, wind for Vatta, bile for Pitta.   These concepts are elaborated on in Bhagavad Gita.
