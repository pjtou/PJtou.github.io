                GUNAS AND DOSHAS      (Pierre Touchard)




Gunas are litterally "thread, string" a "quality" that comes along during the process of incarnation as Purusha, or Spirit, is becoming involved with Prakriti or Nature.   In Shamkhya philosophy, this quality of the Gunas is involved early and at all stages of matter, as the creation is getting more and more cristallized.  The Gunas are in everything and everyone, always there in Nature.   As it were, Shamkhya concepts are imbeded in the early astrology texts of Parasara (Brihat Parasara Hora Shastra) and the Yavana of Spujidhava, 1st century AD.  This suggests that astrology depicts how planets and signs are made of or colored by the creative process of the Gunas, but also, are part of that creative process.  

In some translations of Parasara the word Shakti is used for each Guna, showing it to be a creative force.   Bear in mind that planets are termed Grahas in the Puranas, and Surya Siddantha which are deities, not material planets. 

Parasara (date unknown, but Parasara is purported to be one of the 18 sages who gave astrology to mankind) and Spujidhava are the earliest extant texts giving Gunas and Doshas for each planet. Mantreswhara in 13th century is very close to Parasara.  In this table, in block letter are planets identical for both authors. The text of Spujidhava follows. Some authors derive Vata from Sattva which is how I placed Vata below Sattva, Pitta below Rajas and Kapha below Tamas logically. 

To better follow, Phelgm is the traditional translated word for Kapha, wind for Vatta, bile for Pitta.   These translations are approximate however.  These concepts are elaborated on in Bhagavad Gita. 


Sattva is said to be a quality or property of balance, harmony, virtue.  It is in Nature the closest to the harmony of Purusha, without being Purusha. 

Raja is of passion, excitment, the involvment of Ahamkar (ego) that want results and lives in the mist of thinking it creates, causes the creation (wereas only God creates).  Raja is lively, initiatiatory, active in the modern sense.

Tamas is a dense quality, heavy, the lowest of the 3.  It causes stagnation, inertia, and due to it being anxious, it is involved with problems.  However it is necessary to deal with problems.
