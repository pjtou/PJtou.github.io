GUNAS & DOSHAS

        The Gunas are litterally "a thread, a string" a "quality" that comes in Nature during the process of incarnation.   According to Shamkhya philosophy, Purusha, or Spirit, is the true Self.   Purusha  gets involved with Prakriti or Nature, and with Prakriti comes the Gunas, coloring creation.   In Shamkhya there are 2 main aspects, the Self represents the identity or spiritual essence of life, while Nature is the obvious visible, perceivable aspect. 

So the quality of the Gunas is part of Prakriti or Nature only.   It is not part of the Self, or the real identity of human beings.  So quite early during the creation of Nature, the Gunas begin to act and color and give qualities to creation.  The Gunas are said to be in everything and everyone, always there in Nature.   It is rarely discussed, but Shamkhya concepts are imbeded in the early astrology texts of Parasara (Brihat Parasara Hora Shastra) and the Yavana of Spujidhava, 1st century AD.  This suggests that astrology depicts how the deities of planets and signs are made of and colored by the creative process of the Gunas, but also, that the planets are part of the creative process of Nature.    We can also see that planets color Nature.   And because Nature can be perceived in a large part by the senses, the Gunas might also be, to some extent, perceivable by the senses. 


In some translations of Parasara the word Shakti is used for each Guna, showing them to be a creative force.   Bear in mind that planets are termed Grahas in the Puranas, in Surya Siddantha, and Grahas are actual deities, not material planets.  This raises many questions about astrology, but we will write about this at other times.  Early in his text Parasara states that Graha's purpose is to give humans the fruits of their Karma, and there is stated in a nutshell the fundamental nature of astrology, which is to reveal the karmas of humans. 

Parasara (date unknown, but Parasara is purported to be one of the 18 sages who gave astrology to mankind) and Spujidhava are the earliest extant texts giving Gunas and Doshas for each planet. Mantreswhara in the 13th century is very close to Parasara.  


So we can remember that Gunas give specific qualities to Grahas or Planets.  In this table, in block letter are planets identical for both authors.  The text of Spujidhava follows.  

The table below the Gunas, is the table of the Doshas.  Some authors have the Doshas aligned to the Gunas, so Vata is derived or linked with Sattva which is how I placed Vata below Sattva, Pitta below Rajas and Kapha below Tamas to follow on this idea. 


A word on translation.  For Doshas, Phelgm is the traditional word in the early middle age for Kapha, wind is Vatta, bile for Pitta.   These translations are approximate, as these have mostly a medical or phisiological conotation, wereas the Doshas seem to also have a spiritual and psychological quality to them, or at least, are not as easy to define materialy as we are used.   Early Indian concepts are often tricky to properly translate with occidental words of a more materialistic culture.  

These concepts are elaborated on in Bhagavad Gita, The Upanishads and the Puranas, and the earliest trace I saw of these concepts is in Shamkhya.  ( I say earliest because since the Gita often discusses the Gunas, they must have been taught before the writing of the Gita).


Sattva is said to be a quality or property of balance, harmony, virtue.  It is in Nature the closest to the serenity and harmony of Purusha, without being Purusha, because anything of Prakriti is denser and material and has attachement as a primary nature. 


Raja is of passion, excitment, the involvment of Ahamkar (ego) that want results and lives in the mist of thinking it creates, that is is causing creation (wereas only God creates).  So Raja is lively, initiatiatory, active in the modern sense, passionate, emotional.


Tamas is a dense quality, it is heavy, the lowest vibration of the 3.  It causes stagnation, inertia, and due to it being anxious, it is involved with problems.  However it is necessary to deal with problems in life, so it can be suggested that Tamas shouldnt be judged as bad as many people often do.


In Bhagavad Gita, 14-5, Krishna explains to the eager student Arjuna one fundamental feature of the Gunas :

"Oh Arjuna, what binds the imperishable Soul to the body are the 3 Gunas, brought into manifestation by Prakriti (nature)."


In other words, these qualities of the Gunas bind, impel, the soul to nature and its transient expressions. They propell the attachment to the manifestations in the many physical passions of nature. Even Sattva, the most noble Guna, is binding to nature, to physicality, for instance when intellect becomes too much a source of identification and joy purely of the Ego, and not of the non-separate great Soul.

Spujidhava :

114. Jupiter, Mars, and the Sun are of excellence (sattva), Venus and the Moon of passion (rajas), and Saturn of ignorance (tamas); Mercury accepts the other characteristics depending on which planet it is in conjunction with.


The differences in Spujidhava are intriguing, once again the moon which was associated with Ahamkar, ego in Parasara, is given an emotional tint, that of Rajas, which believes it is the creator, so this actually IS close to the notion of ego.  Bear in mind that the moon never was a romantic emotional sweet planet in antiquity.  That notion in quite modern.  But there are several cultures where the moon was associated with a terrible and malevolent creature manipulating humans.  In south Africa it is the Chitahauri.  Once the moon appeared, brought about by some creatures, the sun began to scorch our human skins, the weather became harsher, hotter, more extreme, and women started to menstruate.  There are similar tales in ancient Pre-Selene Greece, meaning Greece before the moon was there...  They also remember that before the Moon was there women were without menstrual blood.  And we know how the month (close etymology to the moon) as a lunar cycle ties in with women.



The Sun is said to be the soul, or rather it is most probably the self, and as such represents the most Sattvic planet.  Then Jupiter in its pursuit of truth and right knowledge, because Jupiter is the adviser, philosopher, Brahmin planet.  Third moon, and this is because the moon is Ahamkara, meaning the separate sense of oneself as disctinct from others, which IS Ego.  Still the moon for Parasara will show concerns for the true Self, in the maner of the Queen (moon) being always close to and faithful to the wishes of the King (sun). 

For Spujidhava Mars is also Sattvic, and this maybe a surprise for some.   The significance it may have to me is that Mars still represents the right duty of the Overseer that is Mars, which sees to problems and makes sacrifices, perhaps of his life in combat, to the King, in order to preserve and save from any ennemies and threats the country has.  Arjuna in the Bhagavad Gita is from the warrior class, a sun and mars class, and so has to fight and not skirt the issue of the battle.   Mars has a role close to that of the King (Sun) with whom it is mutual friend as a planet.   This shows a noble quality to mars.  


Venus and mercury are Rajas, and show the passions of love, learning, acquiring knowledge and playing games, the excitments of courships, beauty, the arts, the love and passions of life.  They motivate one to desire and progress also. 

The Moon is Rajas for Spujidhava, probably expressing here the Ahamkara, egoic quality of the moon, which sees itself as separate distinct from others and as such maybe blinded.   It also thinks thru the illusory nature of the Ego, which believes that it is creating things, whereas the only creator of anything is Krishna, or God, or the True Self. 


The Tamasic planets are Saturn and Mars, which deal with problems in life.  Limitation, laziness, inertia, but also excessive use of force and lust which are dangers for Mars.  These features can bind the person to matter, physicality, inertia, and hinder the true perception of ones true not separate Self.
